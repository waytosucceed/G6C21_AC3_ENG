Punctuations

Convention/Grammar

A punctuation mark is defined as “a sign or mark used in writing to divide sentences and phrases”. Full stops/periods create clear breaks between complete thoughts. Commas help organise information within sentences. Exclamation marks add emotion or urgency to statements. Question marks indicate when information is being requested.

Let’s take a look at this table:

Punctuation marks Name Usage Example
. Full Stop/Period Ends a sentence with a complete thought, in general, a statement. The fish tank is clean.
, Comma Separates items in a list; joins clauses; sets off extra information. I bought fish food, plants, and decorations for the aquarium.
! Exclamation Mark Shows strong emotion, excitement, or emphasis. Wow! The fish are so colourful!
? Question Mark Ends a direct question; expresses doubt. How often should I clean the fish tank?

Words that I learnt from the story

Equipment: The necessary items for a particular purpose.
Sentence: The team checked their camping equipment before leaving.

Gravel: Small stones and pebbles, often used to cover roads or paths.
Sentence: The driveway was covered in gravel.

Complicated: Difficult to analyse, understand, or explain.
Sentence: The instructions for assembling the furniture were complicated.
